Key Concepts

Learning is not just about absorbing information - it's about making connections and building understanding. Here are the fundamental principles:

Active Recall

Testing yourself on material is more effective than passive review. This technique strengthens neural pathways and improves long-term retention.

Related: Spaced Repetition | Memory Techniques

Interleaving

Mix different topics or skills in a single study session. This approach helps develop discrimination skills and improves problem-solving abilities.

See also: Study Techniques | Cognitive Science

The Pomodoro Technique

  • Work for 25 minutes focused
  • Take a 5-minute break
  • Repeat 4 times
  • Take a longer 15-30 minute break

Reference: Time Management | Productivity Methods

Practical Applications

  1. Create summary sheets after each study session
  2. Teach the concept to someone else (Feynman Technique)
  3. Apply the knowledge to real-world problems
  4. Review material at spaced intervals (1 day, 1 week, 1 month)
