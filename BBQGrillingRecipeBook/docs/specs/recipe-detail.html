Texas-Style Brisket

The king of Texas BBQ. This recipe produces tender, juicy brisket with a beautiful smoke ring and that coveted bark. Low and slow is the name of the game - patience and temperature control are your best friends.

ü•ò Ingredients

  • 1 whole beef brisket (12-14 lbs)
  • 1/4 cup coarse black pepper
  • 1/4 cup kosher salt
  • 2 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp onion powder
  • 1 tbsp paprika
  • Oak or hickory wood chunks
  • Butcher paper for wrapping
üí° Pro Tips
Temperature Probe: Insert probe into thickest part of the flat. Target internal temp is 203¬∞F.

The Stall: Don't panic when temp plateaus around 165¬∞F. This is normal. Wrap in butcher paper to power through.

Rest Time: Let brisket rest for at least 1 hour in a cooler before slicing.

üìã Instructions

  1. 1
    Trim the brisket, removing excess fat and leaving about 1/4 inch fat cap. Square up the edges for even cooking.
  2. 2
    Mix all dry rub ingredients in a bowl. Apply the rub generously to all sides of the brisket. Let sit at room temperature for 1 hour.
  3. 3
    Preheat your smoker to 225¬∞F. Add oak or hickory wood chunks for smoke. Use a water pan to maintain moisture.
  4. 4
    Place brisket fat-side up on the smoker. Insert a temperature probe into the thickest part of the flat, avoiding fat.
  5. 5
    Smoke unwrapped until internal temperature reaches 165¬∞F (about 6-8 hours). Maintain steady 225¬∞F smoker temp. Spritz with water every 2 hours.
  6. 6
    Wrap brisket tightly in butcher paper once it hits 165¬∞F. This helps push through "the stall" and develops the bark.
  7. 7
    Continue cooking wrapped until internal temperature reaches 203¬∞F (about 6 more hours). Probe should slide in like butter.
  8. 8
    Remove from smoker and rest in a cooler (still wrapped) for at least 1 hour, up to 4 hours. This is crucial for moisture retention.
  9. 9
    Unwrap, slice against the grain (direction changes between flat and point). Serve immediately and enjoy!
