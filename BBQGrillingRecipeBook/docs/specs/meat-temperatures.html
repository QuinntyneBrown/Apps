üí° Temperature Pro Tips

üå°Ô∏è Thermometer Placement
Insert the probe into the thickest part of the meat, away from bone and fat. For whole birds, check both breast and thigh - thigh should be 10¬∞F higher.
‚è∞ Carryover Cooking
Temperature continues to rise 5-15¬∞F after removing from heat. For steaks, pull off grill 5¬∞F before target. For large roasts, pull 10-15¬∞F before target.
üéØ Instant-Read is Best
Invest in a quality instant-read thermometer (Thermapen, ThermoWorks). Leave-in probe thermometers are great for long smokes, but instant-read gives most accurate spot checks.
üìç Multiple Checks
Check temperature in multiple spots, especially for larger cuts. The thickest part takes longest to cook. Don't rely on just one reading.
üßä Cold Meat Matters
Let meat come to room temp for 30-60 minutes before cooking for more even doneness. Cold centers create overcooked exteriors.
‚ö†Ô∏è Food Safety First
Ground meats, poultry, and pork MUST reach minimum safe temperatures. Whole muscle cuts like steak can be cooked to lower temps as bacteria is only on the surface.
