Beneficiary Guidelines

  • Choose trusted individuals: Select people who are reliable, tech-savvy enough to handle digital accounts, and whom you trust completely with sensitive information.
  • Discuss their role: Have a conversation with each beneficiary about their responsibilities and make sure they're comfortable with the role.
  • Verify contact information: Keep beneficiary contact details up to date. They'll receive important notifications about their designation.
  • Assign appropriate access levels: Not everyone needs full access to everything. Consider what each person should realistically manage.
  • Designate backups: Have multiple beneficiaries in case your primary executor is unavailable when needed.
  • Review regularly: Life changes - marriages, divorces, fallouts. Review your beneficiary list at least once a year.
