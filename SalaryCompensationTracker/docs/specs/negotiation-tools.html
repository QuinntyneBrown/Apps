Negotiation Scripts & Templates

Initial Offer Response (Asking for Time)
When you first receive an offer and need time to evaluate
"Thank you so much for the offer! I'm very excited about the opportunity to join [Company] and contribute to [specific project/team]. I'd like to take a day or two to review the complete package and discuss with my family. Would it be possible to schedule a follow-up conversation on [specific date]?"
Counter-Offer for Higher Base Salary
When negotiating for a higher base salary
"I'm genuinely excited about this role and the opportunity to work with the team. Based on my research of market rates for this position in [location], along with my [X years] of experience in [specific skills], I was expecting a base salary closer to $[target amount]. Given my track record of [specific achievement], would there be flexibility to adjust the base salary to $[counter amount]?"
Negotiating Multiple Components
When you want to negotiate base, equity, and bonus
"I really appreciate the offer and I'm excited about the role. I've done some analysis comparing the total compensation package to market rates and my current compensation. I'd like to discuss a few adjustments: (1) Base salary increase to $[amount], (2) Equity grant of [shares/amount], and (3) A signing bonus to offset unvested equity I'm leaving behind. Would you be open to discussing these components?"
Using Competing Offer as Leverage
When you have another offer and want to negotiate
"I want to be transparent with you - I'm currently evaluating another offer with a total compensation package of approximately $[amount]. However, [Company] remains my first choice because of [specific reasons]. Is there any flexibility in the compensation package to make this an easier decision for me?"
Accepting with Grace
When you've reached an agreement
"Thank you for working with me on this. I'm thrilled to accept the offer at $[final amount] base salary, [equity details], and [other terms]. I appreciate your flexibility and I'm excited to join the team. When should I expect the formal offer letter, and what are the next steps?"

Build Your Negotiation Leverage

üèÜ
Quantifiable Achievements
Document specific achievements with numbers: "Led migration that reduced infrastructure costs by $200k/year" or "Improved API response time by 60%, supporting 3x user growth." Concrete metrics strengthen your case.
üìä
Market Data
Use platforms like Levels.fyi, Glassdoor, and Blind to research market rates. Reference specific data points: "According to Levels.fyi, the median total compensation for Senior SWE at companies of your size in SF is $245k."
üéØ
Competing Offers
Multiple offers significantly strengthen your position. Even if another offer is lower, it demonstrates market demand for your skills. Always be honest and transparent about competing offers.
üíº
Unique Skills & Experience
Highlight specialized skills that are hard to find: domain expertise, rare technical skills, or experience with specific tools/technologies the company uses. Emphasize how these fill a specific need.
üåü
Cultural Fit & Enthusiasm
While showing enthusiasm, make it clear you're evaluating multiple factors. "I'm very excited about this opportunity, AND I want to make sure the compensation aligns with market rates and my experience."

Negotiation Best Practices

‚úÖ
Always Negotiate
92% of people who negotiate get a higher offer. The worst they can say is no, and most companies expect negotiation.
ü§ê
Never Give First Number
Let them make the first offer. If pressed, provide a wide range based on market research. "I'm looking for something competitive with market rates."
üìä
Focus on Total Comp
Don't fixate only on base salary. Consider equity, bonuses, benefits, PTO, remote work, and learning budgets in your total package evaluation.
‚è∞
Take Your Time
Never accept on the spot. Always ask for 24-48 hours to review, even if you're excited. This shows you're thoughtful and gives you time to strategize.
üìß
Get Everything in Writing
Verbal offers aren't binding. Always request and review a formal written offer letter before accepting or resigning from your current position.
ü§ù
Stay Professional
Negotiation is normal and expected. Stay positive, collaborative, and professional throughout. This is the beginning of your relationship with the company.
