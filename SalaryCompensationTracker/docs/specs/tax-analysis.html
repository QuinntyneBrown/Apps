üí° RSU Tax Strategy
RSUs are taxed as ordinary income when they vest. If you hold the shares after vesting and they appreciate, you'll pay long-term capital gains tax (15%) on the appreciation if held for over 1 year. This can be advantageous if you expect the stock to appreciate. However, holding introduces concentration risk. Consider diversifying by selling at vest and investing in a diversified portfolio.
