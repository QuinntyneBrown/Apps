Tasting Notes

Appearance: Deep garnet-purple color with exceptional clarity and brilliant ruby highlights at the rim. The wine shows excellent viscosity with pronounced legs indicating high alcohol and glycerol content.

Nose: Incredibly complex and layered aromatics. Initial impressions reveal ripe blackcurrant, black cherry, and plum. As the wine opens, secondary notes emerge: cedar, tobacco leaf, graphite, and subtle violet florals. Tertiary aromas include leather, earth, and a touch of vanilla from oak aging. The bouquet is perfectly integrated and harmonious.

Palate: Full-bodied with remarkable elegance and finesse. The attack is silky and refined, leading to a mid-palate bursting with dark fruit flavors - blackberry, cassis, and dark chocolate. Exceptional balance between fruit concentration, acidity, and tannin structure. The tannins are firm yet velvety, providing excellent aging potential. Long, persistent finish with notes of espresso, cedar, and sweet spice lasting 60+ seconds.

Overall Impression: A truly exceptional wine showcasing the pinnacle of Bordeaux winemaking. Currently drinking beautifully but has decades of aging potential ahead. The 2015 vintage shows perfect ripeness without over-extraction, maintaining classic Margaux elegance and structure.
