Homemade Margherita Pizza

This classic Italian Margherita pizza features a crispy homemade crust topped with tangy tomato sauce, creamy fresh mozzarella, and aromatic basil leaves. Simple ingredients come together to create an authentic taste of Italy right in your kitchen. Perfect for family dinners or entertaining guests!

üìã Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 475¬∞F (245¬∞C). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven to heat up for at least 30 minutes. This will help create a crispy crust.

  2. Roll out the pizza dough on a well-floured surface to your desired thickness (about 12 inches in diameter for thin crust). Transfer to a pizza peel or parchment-lined baking sheet.

  3. In a small bowl, mix crushed tomatoes with minced garlic, 1 tablespoon olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread this sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a 1-inch border for the crust.

  4. Tear the fresh mozzarella into small pieces and distribute evenly over the sauce. Don't overload the pizza - you want a nice balance of cheese and sauce.

  5. Drizzle the remaining olive oil over the pizza. Carefully transfer to the preheated oven or pizza stone. Bake for 10-12 minutes until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is bubbly and lightly browned.

  6. Remove from oven and immediately top with fresh basil leaves. Let the pizza cool for 2-3 minutes before slicing. This allows the cheese to set slightly and makes slicing easier. Serve hot and enjoy!
