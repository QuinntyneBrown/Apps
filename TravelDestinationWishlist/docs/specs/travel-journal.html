Chasing the Northern Lights
Nature Adventure Photography Aurora
Tonight was absolutely magical! After three nights of cloudy skies, we finally saw the Northern Lights at Thingvellir National Park. The aurora started as a faint green glow on the horizon, but within minutes it exploded into dancing ribbons of green and purple across the entire sky. I stood there in the freezing cold, completely mesmerized, watching nature's most incredible light show. No photo can truly capture the feeling of witnessing this in person - the way the lights move and shift, the complete silence broken only by occasional gasps of awe from other viewers. This is why I travel. This is what makes all the planning and saving worthwhile.

Sunrise at Mount Batur
Adventure Hiking Sunrise Nature
Woke up at 2 AM for the Mount Batur sunrise hike - probably the earliest I've ever gotten up for anything! The two-hour climb in the dark was challenging but our guide was amazing. As we reached the summit, the sky started changing from black to deep blue to orange. Watching the sun rise over the volcanic landscape with Lake Batur below was absolutely worth the early wake-up call. We had breakfast at the top - eggs cooked in volcanic steam! Such a unique experience. The descent revealed incredible views of the surrounding countryside that we couldn't see on the way up.
Day Highlights
  • Summited Mount Batur at sunrise (1,717m elevation)
  • Breakfast cooked using volcanic steam
  • Met hikers from Australia, Germany, and Japan
  • Coffee plantation visit on the way back
