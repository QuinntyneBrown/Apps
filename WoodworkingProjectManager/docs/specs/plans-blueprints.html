Build Instructions

1
Mill and Prepare Lumber
Select and mill all lumber to final dimensions. Joint one face and edge, plane to thickness, and rip to final width. Allow wood to acclimate for 24-48 hours before proceeding.
2
Create Mortises in Table Legs
Layout and cut mortises on two adjacent faces of each leg. Mortises should be 1" wide √ó 3" long √ó 1" deep, centered on the leg faces. Use drill press and chisel for clean, square mortises.
3
Cut Tenons on Apron Pieces
Create matching tenons on both ends of all apron pieces. Tenons should be slightly undersized for a snug fit. Test fit each joint and adjust as needed with chisel or shoulder plane.
4
Glue Up Tabletop Panel
Arrange tabletop boards for best grain match and color continuity. Apply glue to edges and clamp firmly with even pressure. Use cauls to keep panel flat. Allow 24 hours to cure completely.
5
Assemble Base Structure
Dry fit entire base assembly to ensure proper fit. Apply glue to mortise and tenon joints and assemble base. Check for square and allow to cure. Install corner blocks for additional strength.
6
Surface and Attach Tabletop
Flatten tabletop with hand plane or sander. Cut and fit breadboard ends with elongated mortises to allow seasonal movement. Attach top to base using Z-clip fasteners, allowing for wood expansion.
7
Sand and Finish
Sand entire table progressing through grits 120, 180, 220, and 320. Remove all dust. Apply danish oil finish with lint-free cloth, allowing proper drying time between coats. Apply 3-4 coats for best protection and appearance.
