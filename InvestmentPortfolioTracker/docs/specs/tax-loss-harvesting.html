What is Tax-Loss Harvesting?

Tax-loss harvesting is a strategy where you sell investments at a loss to offset capital gains and reduce your tax liability. You can then reinvest in a similar (but not identical) asset to maintain your portfolio allocation while harvesting the tax benefit. We automatically identify opportunities and suggest replacement securities to help you maximize tax savings while avoiding wash sales.

Wash Sale Rule Protection
All suggested replacements are checked to avoid wash sale violations. The IRS wash sale rule prevents you from claiming a loss if you buy a "substantially identical" security within 30 days before or after the sale. Our recommendations use correlation analysis to suggest similar but not identical securities.

How to Execute

1. Select Opportunities
Review the opportunities table and select positions you want to harvest. Consider your overall tax situation and capital gains for the year.
2. Sell Losing Position
Execute the sale of the underperforming security. The realized loss will offset your capital gains and reduce taxable income.
3. Buy Replacement (Optional)
Immediately purchase the suggested replacement security to maintain your portfolio allocation and market exposure while avoiding wash sale rules.
4. Document for Taxes
We'll track all transactions and generate Form 8949 documentation for your tax filing. Your tax savings will be realized when you file your return.
