Barbell Bench Press

Compound Chest Barbell Intermediate

The barbell bench press is a fundamental upper body compound exercise that primarily targets the pectoralis major, anterior deltoids, and triceps. It's one of the "big three" powerlifting movements and essential for building upper body strength and mass.

How to Perform

Setup

  1. Lie flat on the bench with your eyes directly under the barbell
  2. Plant your feet firmly on the floor, slightly wider than hip-width
  3. Create a slight arch in your lower back while keeping your glutes on the bench
  4. Grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder-width with a full grip (thumbs wrapped around)
  5. Unrack the bar and position it directly over your chest with arms fully extended

Execution

  1. Take a deep breath and begin lowering the bar in a controlled manner
  2. Lower the bar to your mid-chest, keeping your elbows at approximately 45 degrees from your body
  3. Touch the bar lightly to your chest without bouncing
  4. Drive the bar back up explosively, pressing through your chest and triceps
  5. Lock out your elbows at the top without overextending
  6. Repeat for desired number of repetitions

Breathing

  1. Inhale deeply at the top of the movement before lowering
  2. Hold your breath during the descent and initial portion of the press
  3. Exhale forcefully as you complete the pressing motion

üí° Form Tips

  • Keep your shoulder blades retracted (pulled back) throughout the entire movement
  • Maintain constant tension in your upper back and lats to create a stable base
  • Drive your feet into the floor to engage your legs and create full-body tension
  • The bar path should be slightly diagonal, moving from mid-chest to over your shoulders
  • Focus on pressing the bar back, not just up, to engage the chest optimally
  • Control the eccentric (lowering) phase - don't let the bar drop

  • Bouncing the bar off your chest - this can cause injury and reduces muscle engagement
  • Flaring elbows out to 90 degrees - increases shoulder strain and injury risk
  • Lifting your glutes off the bench during the press
  • Using a "suicide grip" (thumbs not wrapped) which can lead to the bar slipping
  • Pressing the bar straight up instead of back towards your shoulders
  • Not using a spotter when working with heavy weights
