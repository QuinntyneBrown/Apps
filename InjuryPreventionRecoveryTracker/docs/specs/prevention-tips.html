Proper Warm-Up & Cool-Down

Foundation of injury prevention

Never skip your warm-up! A proper warm-up increases blood flow, raises muscle temperature, and prepares your body for activity. Cool-downs help remove waste products and reduce muscle soreness.

  • Spend 5-10 minutes on dynamic stretches before exercise
  • Include sport-specific movements in your warm-up
  • Cool down with 5-10 minutes of light activity
  • Follow up with static stretching after workouts

Strength & Flexibility Balance

Build resilient muscles and joints

Muscle imbalances are a leading cause of injuries. Maintain a balanced strength and flexibility program that addresses all major muscle groups, especially opposing muscle pairs.

  • Train opposing muscle groups equally (e.g., quads and hamstrings)
  • Include both strength training and flexibility work
  • Address weak links identified in your injury history
  • Incorporate single-leg exercises for balance
üìà

Progressive Overload

Gradual intensity increases

Doing too much, too soon is a recipe for injury. Follow the 10% rule: don't increase training volume, intensity, or duration by more than 10% per week.

  • Increase training load gradually over time
  • Allow adaptation periods between intensity increases
  • Listen to your body's warning signs
  • Plan periodic recovery weeks

Rest & Recovery

Essential for adaptation

Recovery is when your body adapts and becomes stronger. Inadequate rest leads to overtraining, fatigue, and increased injury risk.

  • Get 7-9 hours of quality sleep per night
  • Include rest days in your training schedule
  • Use active recovery techniques (foam rolling, light cardio)
  • Manage stress levels for optimal recovery
üéØ

Proper Form & Technique

Quality over quantity

Poor form puts unnecessary stress on joints and tissues, dramatically increasing injury risk. Always prioritize proper technique over heavier weights or more repetitions.

  • Work with a qualified coach to learn proper form
  • Use mirrors or video to check your technique
  • Reduce weight if you can't maintain good form
  • Address mobility limitations that affect form
